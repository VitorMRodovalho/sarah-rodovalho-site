Public-transit infrastructure carries every constraint civil engineering can
offer at once: existing right-of-way conflicts, eminent-domain procedures,
multi-jurisdictional review, and a fixed political schedule.

Within a two-month critical-criteria window, the design-management workstream
resolved key right-of-way conflicts through targeted re-routing and
alignment revisions — bypassing several eminent-domain challenges while
realizing R$1.5M in design-change savings.
