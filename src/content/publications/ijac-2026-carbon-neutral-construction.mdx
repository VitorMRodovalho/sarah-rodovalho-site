abstract: >
  This study investigates how Building Information Modeling (BIM) and energy
  analysis tools can be integrated into early-stage design decisions to support
  the transition toward carbon-neutral construction. By analyzing design models
  and their environmental implications—including CO₂ emissions—the research
  proposes a workflow that informs material and system choices when their
  embodied- and operational-carbon impact is most adjustable.
