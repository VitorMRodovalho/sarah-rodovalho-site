abstract: >
  This paper presents a comprehensive life-cycle assessment (LCA) of CO₂
  emissions across Construction Specifications Institute (CSI) categories,
  identifying the carbon footprint of different building systems and materials.
  The results show that concrete divisions consistently emerge as the predominant
  contributors to global warming potential, exceeding 75% in several instances—
  pointing to where material substitution and embodied-carbon mitigation deliver
  the largest emissions reductions across construction phases.
